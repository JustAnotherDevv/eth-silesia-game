 The rubber hose 1930s aesthetic is already locked. Now — what shape does the platform take?

  ---
  Option A — "Coin Capers" — the Episode Structure

  Think old cartoon episode format. Each session = one episode, ~5 minutes. You pick a cartoon character at signup (rubber hose style: a frog banker, a
  cat accountant, a dog hustler). Each episode drops you into a scenario — "Your landlord wants 3 months upfront. You have a PKO account. What do you
  do?" — with branching decisions and a laugh track moment at the end. XP = your "star rating" per episode. Replayable. Very narrative.

  Vibe: Cuphead meets Duolingo

  ---
  Option B — "The Daily Gazette" — newspaper front page as dashboard

  Every day a new "edition" drops. The front page is your dashboard — cartoon newspaper style, fake headlines, each article is a challenge. "SAVINGS
  ACCOUNT MYSTERY: Can YOU solve the compound interest caper?" Completing a challenge = you get your byline printed. Very immersive in the aesthetic,
  each day feels like an event.

  Vibe: Papers Please meets financial literacy, Fleischer Studios newspaper room

  ---
  Option C — "The XP Arcade" — literal arcade cabinet metaphor

  Your dashboard is a 1930s arcade parlor. Each machine = a different challenge type. Quiz machine, Decision machine, Savings simulator machine. You put
   a coin in (your daily energy), play the round, win tickets, redeem for badges/NFTs. Very modular — easy to add new "machines" over time.

  Vibe: old penny arcade, very visual and spatial, works great with the aesthetic

  ---
  Option D — "The Counting House" — city builder lite

  You manage a tiny 1930s cartoon town. Your financial decisions grow the town — make good choices and new buildings appear, bad choices and buildings
  wobble and fall over (rubber hose squash). The town IS your portfolio/progress visualization. PKO products = the buildings (savings account = a bank
  building, insurance = a fire station, etc.).

  Vibe: most ambitious, but most memorable demo — city grows live on screen

  ---
  Option E — "Quick Rounds" — pure game show format

  Lean all the way into the game show angle. A rubber hose MC character hosts. You're a contestant. Countdown timer, buzzer, spinning wheel for
  categories. Financial knowledge is the trivia. Multiplayer-friendly — could have a live audience/leaderboard during the hackathon demo itself.

  Vibe: most demo-friendly for a live hackathon presentation
